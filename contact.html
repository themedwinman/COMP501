How Do BCIs Help?

Brain-Computer Interfaces can restore independence for individuals with mobility or communication impairments. For example, BCIs enable patients with ALS to type using only their thoughts or help quadriplegics control robotic limbs.

In mental health, BCIs are being studied as potential treatments for depression, PTSD, and anxiety by modulating neural activity. They're also being tested in virtual reality for therapeutic immersion.

As research progresses, BCIs may improve learning, productivity, and even allow for new types of human-machine creativity and collaboration.
